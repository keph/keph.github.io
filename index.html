A classy chat with a friend

Published: Wt 20 styczeń 2015
By Keph

In en.

tags: fathergasted

I'm not dead, just preoccupied. (How's that for a soundbite bon mot quote sort of deal?). In fact, I've got a lovely bunch of coconutsdraft notes I need to sit down for and finally maybe make happen. That being said, sometimes an opportunity presents itself. Like, say, an IM chat with a friend of mine, whose blog you might be interested in perusing, if you're of the nerdy persuasion. And if you're reading this, odds are you might be.

Anyway, the conversation was about baby farts.

What? I told you it got classy.

Basically, here's a surprising fact you might not be even aware of. I know I wasn't, until I was over at my and my wife's friends' house, who had a baby before we did (PARENTING PROTIP: try and hang out with people with slightly older babies, that'll give you some heads-up on what to look out for in the coming months). They told us the baby was gassy and they're trying to get it to fart it all out.

Now, what we expected was, I don't know, maybe like a whistle sort of sound? What we emphatically did not expect was grown-ass old-man-style rancid dubstep tuba smackdown, which is what happened. Seriously, if I had to pick one answer for "what was the most surprising thing as a new parent?" question, that would be it. Because, as we soon learned from our own experience, all babies are like that. Anyway, here's a bit of the conversation, slightly edited for clarity and maybe an esprit de l'escalier or two:

Fuzzums: I think that's just to remind you that he's alive and well
Me: I actually laughed at the idea irl. And I guess that might have actually happened already? Like when you're a real fresh parent and are like YO CHECK OUT THE BABY IF EVERYTHINGS OKAY IS IT STILL BREATHING OH GOD OH GOD in the dead of the night

And the baby's like BRRRRRRRRRRTTTTTTTTTTTT Fuzzums: he's like "I either scream or fart. They get all pissy when I scream, so farts it is"
Me:: tbh I'm not sure if farts are connected to any particular emotional state at that developmental stage, not even the inherent hilarity
Fuzzums: yeah, doubt it myself. but still
